Biography

Bailey believes fervently in the power of language to change the world. An english major at Columbia University, she came to a stark realization after graduation that career opportunities for english majors were few and far between.

Undiscouraged by dismal odds, Bailey found her way giving voice to organizations and brands on digital media. With a knack for distilling complex issues into sharp calls-to-action , Bailey has crafted messaging that provoked policy shifts, raised awareness about social justice issues, and drove online traffic.

The only middle schooler both kicked out of class for being “too political” and to dress up as a judge for Halloween (think: RBG before she was ‘hip’), she cares passionately about issues like criminal justice reform, the constitutional right to digital privacy, and a woman's right to choose. Professionally, she works to move criminal justice forward through her digital communications role at the District Attorney's office.

When she isn't writing social media copy, she is listening to NPR, baking kick-ass homemade cinnamon rolls, and reading #toomany biographies. A Seattleite at heart, she still gets nostalgic during rain storms and enjoys a sharp cup of coffee.
